About

I'm Kayleigh Gomez, a public health professional focused on long-term health behavior change and exercise adherence for women. My background in public health informs everything I create—from frameworks to training programs.

I work exclusively with women because the mainstream fitness industry largely ignores how women's bodies, lives, and constraints differ from men's. My approach centers on translating research into practical, sustainable systems that fit real life.

I believe fitness should be repeatable, not extreme. The programs and tools I design prioritize adherence over intensity, sustainability over quick results, and education over dependency. My goal is to help women build confidence through understanding—not just following instructions.

Principles

  • Fitness should be repeatable, not extreme
  • Consistency beats intensity
  • Programs should adapt to people
  • Education creates confidence
  • Long-term behavior change > short-term outcomes

Scope & Boundaries

My work focuses on education and program design guidance only. I do not provide medical diagnosis, treatment, or individualized health advice. The content I publish is educational and should not replace consultation with qualified healthcare professionals. If you have specific health concerns, please consult a licensed provider.

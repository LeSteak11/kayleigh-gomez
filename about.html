About Kayleigh Gomez

Public health–informed publishing focused on exercise adherence, behavior change, and women's fitness.

Positioning

I'm Kayleigh Gomez, a public health graduate working at the intersection of exercise adherence, behavior change, and women's fitness.

My work focuses on why most fitness systems fail to support long-term consistency—and how programs can be designed to function under real-world constraints rather than ideal conditions.

This platform exists to publish research-informed frameworks, applied tools, and long-form thinking about sustainable exercise behavior in women. It is not a coaching service, a medical practice, or an influencer brand.

Programs should adapt to people—not the other way around.
Background

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Public Health and have a professional background in movement instruction, wellness education, and clinical-adjacent care environments.

My experience spans community health, patient support, mobility and corrective exercise, and the design of asynchronous wellness resources. Across these settings, my focus has remained consistent: translating health and movement science into systems people can realistically maintain over time.

Publishing Philosophy

This work approaches fitness through public health, behavior change, and systems thinking rather than optimization or intensity. Consistency matters more than effort.

All published material prioritizes clarity, ethical claims, and applied usefulness. Research informs the work, but is translated thoughtfully rather than cited performatively.
